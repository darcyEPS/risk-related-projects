## Key Terms

- **Risk** is the potential loss of life, injury, or damage that could occur to a community or system.

- **Risk** depends on three factors: **hazard** (a natural event or process that can cause harm), **exposure** (people, assets, or ecosystems that could be affected), and **vulnerability** (how easily those exposed elements can be harmed).

- **Resilience** is the ability of a community or system to resist, absorb, adapt to, and recover from hazards efficiently, while maintaining essential functions.

- **Ecosystem services** are the benefits that healthy ecosystems provide to people—such as clean water, flood regulation, and cultural value. These principles are reflected in *Hílekw Sq'eq'ó* and Ducks Unlimited's *International Science Report 2025*, which emphasize conserving and strengthening natural systems for long-term resilience.

## Sendai Priorities

According to the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction (2015–2030):

- **Priority 1:** Understanding disaster risk.
- **Priority 2:** Strengthening disaster risk governance to manage disaster risk.
- **Priority 3:** Investing in disaster reduction for resilience.
- **Priority 4:** Enhancing disaster preparedness for effective response, and to "Build Back Better" in recovery, rehabilitation, and reconstruction.
